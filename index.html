6 Away

My entry for the MIT 6.470 web programming competition. 6 Away provides a unique way to visualize the network of people around you and easily see who are the people you could be friends with. After registering and filling out of basic information about themselves, they can explore the "social graph" surrounding them with the size of a node related to how similar you are in thinking.

Multi Touch Screens

After watching a video of Jeff Han's wall–sized multi touch screen, I became obsessed with the possibility of making my own. A cheap first prototype was made from a torn apart computer monitor and two infrared lasers. In my high school senior engineering class I led a team to build a multi touch screen desk. These explorations were done before touch screen computers were common and were a way for me to play with the future of interacting with computers.

Conscious Disposal

After being challenged to design a phone system, I set out to provide a solution to the larger problem of recycling: knowing how to dispose of things beyond traditional paper and plastic recycling. The system was designed so that a user could call in and describe an item — any item — and the system would recommend the best way to dispose of it including everything from donation options to trash removal services.

HomePrint

Research done with the Mediated Matter group of the MIT Media Lab. The project explored the possibilities of building scale 3D printing using 3D printed insulation foam molds filled with concrete. I developed HomePrint: a program which was the interface for building designers to turn 3D models of buildings into printed objects.

Mind Painting

A project at the intersection of technology and art. Signals picked up by a subject's brain were read by an EEG and interpreted by a Processing program. This program then controlled motors which directed the flow of dripping paint to create a dynamic painting of the subject's mind.
